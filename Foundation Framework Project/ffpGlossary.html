Glossary of Terms

Like any large group, Maureen's Children and the witch community as a whole, has their own terminology. These words may not be readily apparent to those who aren't experienced with them. This list of terms is not, by any means, a full dictionary of witch-related terminology, but acts as a good reference for the non-initiated.

Bless
(v) The act of putting a magical spell upon an individual with benevolent motive. (Opposite of Curse)
Charm
(n) A beneficial magical spell that is blessed upon an individual who is not the spell caster, usually, but not always, a green person.
Conduit
(n) A witch’s tool that is used to direct magical energy. A conduit is usually a small object that the witch has a strong emotional attachment to, including security blankets, photographs, coins, stuffed animals, etc.
Curse
A magical spell, or the action involving such spell, that is put upon an individual with malevolent motive. (Opposite of Bless or Charm)
Days of Salem
The period of time between October 1999 and March 2004 when all public witches in North America were held in Salem Oregon. During this time tortuous experimentations were enacted on many witches. The Days of Salem were a horrible time for the magical community.
Green
(adj) Describing a non-magical person.
Lavender Child
(n) A witch born to two non-magical parents.
Mother Maureen's Day
(n) A holiday for witches in North America, Mother Maureen’s Day takes place on March 21st each year in remembrance of the passing of Mother Maureen Laurel Descouteaux. This holiday is usually celebrated with large gatherings, festivals, and feasts in the witch community.
Sons and Daughters of Salem
A group that came together during The Days of Salem, The Sons and Daughters of Salem are known for their aggressive attacks against the non-magical community in hopes of gaining the witch community respect. This group’s actions are not advocated by Maureen’s Children.
Spell
(n) Any tangible evidence of magical energy. This includes charms, curses, and magically generated objects.
Witch
(n) Any person, male or female, who has the ability to utilize magical energy.
